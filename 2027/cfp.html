Call For Papers

Scope of the Conference

ICISTA 2027 will be held as a hybrid conference, allowing both in-person and online participation.

ICISTA 2027 serves as a premier international forum for researchers, academics, and industry practitioners to present and discuss the latest advances in intelligent systems, advanced computing, and emerging technologies. The conference aims to foster collaboration and facilitate the exchange of innovative ideas, theoretical developments, and practical applications that address complex real-world challenges in engineering and computational sciences.

Authors are invited to submit original, unpublished research contributions. Topics of interest include, but are not limited to, the following areas:

Track 1: Artificial Intelligence and Applications

  • Machine learning, deep learning, and neural network architectures
  • Generative AI and large language models (LLMs)
  • Computer vision, image understanding, and video analytics
  • Natural language processing and speech processing
  • Reinforcement learning and autonomous decision-making systems
  • Explainable, trustworthy, and ethical AI
  • Multimodal learning and data fusion techniques
  • AI applications in industry, finance, healthcare, and intelligent environments

Track 2: Signal and Image Processing, Electronics, and Embedded Systems

  • Digital, statistical, and adaptive signal processing
  • Image, audio, and multimedia processing
  • Biomedical signal processing and medical image analysis
  • Brain-Computer Interface Technologies
  • Embedded systems design and real-time processing
  • IoT-enabled embedded systems and edge intelligence
  • VLSI design and hardware architectures
  • FPGA-, ASIC-, and microcontroller-based systems
  • Low-power and energy-efficient electronic system design

Track 3: Intelligent Systems, Robotics, and Control

  • Autonomous systems and advanced robotics
  • Control theory, nonlinear control, and intelligent control
  • Industrial automation and smart manufacturing systems
  • Cyber-physical systems and digital twin technologies
  • Human–robot interaction and collaborative robotics
  • Localization, navigation, and SLAM techniques
  • Multi-agent systems and swarm intelligence
  • Intelligent sensing, perception, and actuation systems

Track 4: Communication, Networks, and Security

  • Wireless, mobile, satellite, and optical communication systems
  • 5G/6G, beyond-5G, and future communication technologies
  • Network architectures, optimization, and performance analysis
  • Internet technologies and distributed systems
  • Cybersecurity, cryptography, and secure communication protocols
  • Privacy-preserving communications and data protection
  • Network resilience, reliability, and fault tolerance
  • Blockchain, distributed ledger technologies, and secure applications

Track 5: Smart Systems and Sustainable Technologies

  • Smart cities and intelligent infrastructure systems
  • Digital health, e-health, and healthcare technologies
  • Wearable devices and remote monitoring systems
  • Renewable energy systems and smart grids
  • Energy management, optimization, and energy storage systems
  • Green computing and sustainable ICT solutions
  • Intelligent transportation systems and smart mobility
  • Integrated smart environments and IoT ecosystems

Review Process

All submitted papers will undergo a rigorous peer-review process conducted by international experts in the relevant fields. Each manuscript will be evaluated based on originality, technical quality, research depth, correctness, relevance to the conference scope, contribution to the field, and clarity of presentation. Only papers that meet the highest academic and scientific standards will be accepted for presentation and publication.

Publication

Accepted and presented papers will be included in the conference proceedings. The organizers intend to submit the proceedings for publication in the IEEE Xplore Digital Library, subject to IEEE approval and compliance with quality and formatting requirements.
